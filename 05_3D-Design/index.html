<br> #### Week 5: 3D Design and 3D Printing This week was both incredibly fun and incredibly rewarding for me. Not only did I get to create my very first 3D prints ever- I was able to make my own original design and accomplished some rapid iteration with it. I decided to make a very personal project: a keychain built to hold recovery coins for those in Alcoholics Anonymous. I have a few family members who are involved with a program, and preceding this week I discovered a friend fo mine had chosen to begin the program for the first time. This project served as a great way to make something truly meaningful to me while admittedly distracting me from worrying about my friend. <img src= "IMG_5093.png" width= "300px"> Pictured above is the first iteration. I was vrey proud of myself for my improvements in Fusion360 that allowed me to bring this idea to life. I even successfully created a snap fit mechanism to allow this keychain to encompass a recovery coin. However, this first version did not take into account how PET filament settles and spreads as it cools. The distortion is minute, but this design was small and precise enough that it prevented the two halves from closing completely when the coin was inside. <img src="IMG_5117" width= "150px"> Fortunately, this was an easy enough fix. By making the inner ring both taller and wider by about 2mm, I was able to successfully snap the two halves closed. For good measure, I also made the snap closure on this version go further around the bottom of the coin to make it more secure. Every time I open this keychain, I am always pleasantly surprised how durable the PET is. Opening the snapfit makes it feel like it is going to snap, and it never does. <img src="IMG_5116" width= "300px">
